case study 2016

Transform a cluttered, desktop-only platform into a responsive, mobile-first design, and streamline the user journey to simplify the path from initial site visit to successful pet adoption

My Role: Solo UX/UI Designer, Research, Ideation, Wireframes, High Fidelity Design, Prototyping, UX Testing, Front end development

Completed at lever pulley

  • Target audience is primarily using their phones to search for pets and the site is not mobile friendly.
  • The site is not easy to update for the volunteers and employees at Utah Humane Society
  • Donations and the 10k adoption goal need to be front and center in an engaging way.
  • Create a visually stunning, beautifully branded, mobile first design that will work across all devices.
  • Provide prospective adopters with instant access to the most important portals on the site: adoption, donation, and other services offered
  • Implement an easy to use CMS so that Humane Society of Utah is self reliant for updates
  • Visualize Humane Society of Utah's goal of surpassing 10000 adoptions in a year

Target AudienceCouples in their 20s-40s looking for an animal companion.

  • The target audience was determined from gathering statistics of adoptive pet parents from HSU over a period of time.
  • Humane society websites from across the country were analyzed and broken down into pros and cons as well as widgets. We also looked at who HSU’s biggest “competitors” at other humane societies in other states.

Design Process

1Strategy

A persona was constructed and then I prepared a user journey map of the existing site to help inform what issues would need to be resolved during the design process. From there, and extensive deep dive of other states’ humane society pages was conducted to look for sections to mimic or avoid. Moving forward in the planning stage, a task flow was created for the user to get to adoptions

Heather, 30

Devices: phone mobile desktop

Heather often has to work overtime so she doesn’t have a lot of time to spend searching for the perfect pet. She’s always on some kind of device so she wants to be able to search on whatever she’s holding.

Heather and her wife have very demanding jobs, so they agree that they probably want a cat as they’re more self-reliant. Heather wants an orange cat and her wife wants a black cat, so they want to be able to search for different breeds and be able to easily share pet listings with each other.

Devices: phone mobile desktop

Goals
  • To find companionship with a cuddly orange cat (search by breed)
  • To be able to share prospective pets with her wife (social share)
  • To be able to search on whatever device she is holding (responsive design)
Frustrations
  • Site is ugly and overwhelming
  • Difficult navigation
  • Desktop only
  • Have to manually record kennel number
  • The process to adopt is unclear and hard to find.

2Site Architecture

The site was quite dense with information so as organization began, we chose the most important sections that we would want to uplevel into the main navigation. The rest of the items were organized into a well-organized hamburger nav.

3Wireframing

Wireframing began with a conversation with the client about what the primary goals should be for users. After producing some thumbnail sketches, the client was asked for feedback. The wireframes were adjusted to bring a heavier emphasis on donations.

4Design System

The client’s site had no branding what so ever, so I developed a design system from scratch using the two colours in the logo as the base for the colour palette.

5High Fidelity Design

After the design system was in place and we had the client’s approval. I moved onto the hifi design.

6Prototyping

Prototyping took place in the form of rapid iterations on a staging site hosting the Drupal CMS with live wireframes. Testing took place among the stakeholders and various employees and volunteers at Utah Humane. Feedback was gathered and implemented into future iterations. After the design was completed and approved, it was applied to the drupal site and then shown again

Challenges

  • The Drupal frontend was quite restrictive for intense customization
  • The timeline was quite tight so creating or applying a new bootstrap for the CMS wasn’t an option

Solutions

After some pair-coding on the front end with a Drupal expert on the team, we rapidly experimented until we could push the CMS front end to do what we wanted.

Results

  • HSU successfully reached their goal of over 10000 pets adopted, and then increased the goal multiple times as they reached each new goal.
  • Users reacted positively to the new site, stating that they liked being able to search for pets on their phone and then bring that page right into Utah Humane Society and ask to see that pet, instead of having to write down the pets identification number and use that.
  • Donations increased as users could easily envision what a one time or monthly amount could be used for to help the animals at the shelter.

FutureConsiderations

For future projects, allocating more time for CMS integration and considering potential technical limitations earlier in the design process could streamline development. Additionally, the success of the goal visualization feature suggests that incorporating similar motivational elements could be beneficial in future designs.
